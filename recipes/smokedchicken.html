Smoked Chicken

Description

This is my go-to method for smoking a whole chicken.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut up
  • Kosher salt
  • Coarsely ground black pepper. Other seasonings (paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder) are optional.
  • Wood Chunks or chipped for smoking

Steps

  1. Cut up the chicken, removing innards and backbone. You should have 2 of each part -- breast, thigh, wings, and legs.
  2. Dry brine the chicken with kosher salt about 2-4 hours before cooking.
  3. Setup a clean grill for 2-zone cooking, with a target temp of 275 degrees on the indirect side.
  4. Add seasonings to taste.
  5. Add wood chunks to the grill. About 1 or 2 ounces should be sufficient. Chicken will easily take on the smoky flavors, so less is more in this case.
  6. Add meat to the indirect side of the grill, skin side up, and wait for the temp to reach around 160 degrees. This normally takes 1-2 hours
  7. Open the lid and move the chicken to direct flame. We want to make a nice crust on the skin here and bring the temp up to 165 degrees.
  8. Once the meat is reaches 165 and the skin has a nice crust, remove the chicken and serve immediately!
